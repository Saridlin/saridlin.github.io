About Me

Daria Orlova is a student at San Jose State University currently completing toward a Bachelor's of Fine Arts in Digital Media Art. She will be graduating in 2026 with a focus on digital art, design knowledge and web-based graphics. In a world where technology integrates itself further into our lives with every passing day, there is a growing surrealistic quality to exploring nature; and so her biggest inspiration is the fluidity and liveliness of the natural world.

Primarily trained in watercolor and graphite, she has experience in film photography and has also been publishing digital work online since 2016 as a freelance illustrator. Daria has been involved in creating larger installations, such as the LAVTA Wheels Bus shelters throughout her hometown and the San Pedro Square Market street mural. She has an avid passion for learning various handicrafts whether that is crochet, medieval braiding, origami and anything else fun!
